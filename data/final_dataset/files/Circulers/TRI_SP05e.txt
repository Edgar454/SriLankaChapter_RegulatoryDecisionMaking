FERTILISER RECOMMENDATIONS FOR TEA SEED BEARERS

This Advisory Circular replaces Circular No. F 17, Serial No 07/94, issued in June
1994 on the use of fertiliser for tea seed bearers.

1.0 Fertiliser mixtures for young tea seed bearers
1.1 From field planting to 2nd year
The mixture T200 is recommended for young tea plants.

1.1.1. Composition of T200

100 parts Sulphate of ammonia (20.6% N)
50 parts Eppawela rock phosphate (28.5% P2Os)
25 parts Muriate of potash (60% K,0)
_25 parts Kieserite {24% MgO)
200 parts

The mixture contains approximately 10.3% N, 6.9% P2Os, 7.5% K,O and 3.0%
MgO.

1.14.2 Dosage and frequency of application
Applications can commence approximately two months after planting. Apply
T200 at the rates of 15 or 21 g per plant per application six or four times in the
1st year, and 21 or 32 g per plant per application six or four times in the 2nd
year.

Fertiliser mixture for mature tea seed bearers

The mixture U330 is recommended for mature plants.

Composition of U330

130 parts Urea (46% N)

100 parts Eppawela rock phosphate (28.5% P20.)
100 parts Muriate of potash (60% K20)
330 parts

The mixture contains approximately 18.1% N, 8.6% P.O; and 18.2% KO.
Dosage and frequency of application

Apply U330 at the rate of 275 g per plant per application three times in each
year.

It is important to avoid fertiliser applications in the 3 months prior to flowering.
